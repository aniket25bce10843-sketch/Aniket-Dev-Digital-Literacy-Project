# Cybercrime Case Study - Phishing Attack
# Phishing
## It is one of the most widespread forms of cybercrime and remains highly effective despite widespread awareness campaigns. The main tactic is sending a message to someone which appears to come from a trusted source like any institution or any company or a known contact so that they can trick the recipient into handing over sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal identification data.
---
# The Case Study
## A realistic scenario targeting students goes as follows: a college student receives an email that looks like it has come from a well-known company, offering a paid internship position and assuring to get placed in that company. The email recieved to the student has a professional layout, a convincing subject line along the lines of 'Urgent: Internship Offer And Confirm Placement — Response Required Within 24 Hours', and a link to a form asking for academic details and email login credentials for the verification of the account. The deadline is delibirate — it is designed to stop the recipient from pausing to think critically. The college student gets excited by the opportunity and due to time pressure, fills in the form without checking whether the sender's email address is legitimate or whether the company's official website mentions any such programme. Within hours, they begin receiving notifications of unauthorised login attempts to their accounts.
---
# General Targets
## College Students and youths are frequently targeted because they are actively searching for internship or job opportunities and are statistically more likely to respond quickly.
